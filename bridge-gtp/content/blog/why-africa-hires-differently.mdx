Most companies entering Africa hire the same way they hire in London or New York. They post a role, screen resumes, run a few interviews, and pick the candidate with the best paper.

Then they wonder why it does not work.

## The resume problem

Resumes in Africa do not signal what they signal in mature markets. A good resume in Lagos looks like a good resume in London on paper, but the underlying signal is different. We have seen brilliant operators with thin resumes and weak operators with impressive ones.

If you hire on the resume, you are betting on the wrong signal.

## What actually works

We have placed country managers across Côte d&rsquo;Ivoire, Cameroon, Ghana, and Malawi. The pattern that works is not about credentials. It is about three things:

1. **Operating history under pressure.** Has this person actually run something hard, in this market, with real consequences for failure? Most resumes do not surface this.
2. **Network density.** Who picks up the phone when they call? In Africa, the network is the operating system.
3. **Cultural fluency.** Can they navigate the gap between what is said and what is meant — across languages, regions, and institutions?

These are the things we filter for. They take longer to verify than reading a resume. They are also the things that decide whether the hire works.

## Why this matters now

The companies entering Africa today are more serious than the ones who came five years ago. The bar for talent is higher because the work is harder and the timelines are shorter.

If you are still hiring on resumes, you are losing time.
